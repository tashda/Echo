**Workspace Tabs**

- The Tabs Area (WorkspaceTabStrip) sits directly beneath `TopBarNavigator`, inheriting its horizontal padding and adding a fixed 12 pt inset on each side on top of the configurable leading/trailing padding so the strip stays clear of the window edges; the trailing controls reserve width so tabs never collide with them.  
- The Tabs Background (SafariTabBarBasePlate) is a continuously rounded rectangle with a 15 pt corner radius that extends 4 pt beyond the outermost tabs on both sides; it’s 24 pt tall and draws a subtle stroke plus shadow. The fill is a vertical gradient that mimics Safari: light mode blends `Color(white: 0.96)` to `Color(white: 0.90)`, dark mode uses white at 20 % down to 13 % opacity, and the stroke/shadow also adapt to the color scheme.  
- Each Tab (WorkspaceTabButton) mirrors Safari’s behavior: 15 pt corner radius, 24 pt min height, adaptive padding (19 pt horizontal for regular tabs, 15 pt for pinned), hover-only close control on macOS, middle-click close support, and context actions for pinning, duplication, and bulk closing. Active, hover, drop-target, and pinned states all adjust gradients, borders, and shadows to match the Safari look.  
- Tabs reorder like Safari tabs, honoring pinned tabs at the leading edge. The strip computes the available width after subtracting separators and trailing controls, then divides it evenly so tabs expand to fill the space while maintaining 1 px hairline separators that disappear for the active, hovered, and drag-gap positions.
